D.P.J ABDUL KALAM

1931-2015

Mile Man of India

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, in full Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, (born October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, India—died July 27, 2015,
Shillong), Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs.
He was president of India from 2002 to 2007

BIOGRAPHY

  • Kalam earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology
  • he moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation, where he was project director of the SLV-III
  • Agni, India’s first intermediate-range ballistic missile, which incorporated aspects of the SLV-III and was launched in 1989.
  • In 2002 India’s ruling National Democratic Alliance (NDA)
  • Kalam wrote several books, including an autobiography, Wings of Fire (1999).
